Financial and Insurance Industry

Introduction

This section examines trends in mental health and workplace conditions among employees in the Financial and Insurance Services industry across 2024 and 2025. Using quarterly survey waves, the analysis explores patterns in psychological distress (K6), burnout (BAT-12), SuperFriend domains and psychosocial hazards. Visualisations and statistical tests are used to assess whether these indicators changed across the study period. The aim is to provide a clear overview of workforce mental health within this industry and identify whether notable shifts occurred across the survey waves.

Psychological distress levels show a generally stable pattern across quarters in both 2024 and 2025. In both years, distress increases slightly from Q1 to Q2, suggesting a mild mid year rise. After Q2, levels gradually decline toward Q4, indicating some improvement later in the year. The patterns for 2024 and 2025 are very similar overall, although distress appears slightly higher in 2025 during the first half of the year. The confidence intervals overlap across most quarters, suggesting that differences between years are relatively small. Overall, the results indicate moderate and fairly consistent levels of psychological distress across the study period, with only minimal quarter to quarter variation.

score_sf <- function(df, items) {
  m <- rowMeans(df[, items], na.rm = TRUE)
  s <- (m - 1) * 25
  s[is.nan(m)] <- NA
  s
}

# Hazard items
mgmt_support_items <- c("b2_1","b2_2","b2_3","b2_4","b2_5")

score_hazard <- function(df, items){
  m <- rowMeans(df[, items], na.rm = TRUE)
  s <- m * 20
  s[is.nan(m)] <- NA
  s
}

Inferential test

run_pairwise_ttests <- function(df, outcome) {
  df <- df |>
    select(Quarter, all_of(outcome)) |>
    drop_na()

  means_tbl <- df |>
    group_by(Quarter) |>
    summarise(mean_value = mean(.data[[outcome]]), .groups = "drop")

  pw <- pairwise.t.test(
    x = df[[outcome]],
    g = df$Quarter,
    p.adjust.method = "none"
  ) |>
    broom::tidy()

  pw <- pw |>
    rowwise() |>
    mutate(
      estimate =
        means_tbl$mean_value[means_tbl$Quarter == group2] -
        means_tbl$mean_value[means_tbl$Quarter == group1]
    ) |>
    ungroup() |>
    mutate(outcome = outcome)

  pw
}

outcomes <- c(
  "K6score","BAT12_total",
  "Connectedness","Leadership","Safety",
  "Work_Design","Capability",
  "Hazard_Management_Support","Hazard_Workload","Hazard_Recognition"
)

ttest_finance_all <- bind_rows(
  lapply(c(2024, 2025), function(yr){
    bind_rows(
      lapply(outcomes, function(out){
        run_pairwise_ttests(
          filter(itw_finance, Year == yr),
          out
        ) |>
          mutate(Year = yr)
      })
    )
  })
)

ttest_finance_all <- ttest_finance_all |>
  group_by(Year, outcome) |>
  mutate(
    p_adj = p.adjust(p.value, method = "bonferroni"),
    significant = p_adj < 0.05
  ) |>
  ungroup()

Conclusion

Overall, the results indicate that mental health and workplace conditions in the Financial and Insurance Services industry remained relatively stable across the 2024–2025 period. Psychological distress and burnout showed only minimal variation across quarters, with similar patterns observed in both years. Organisational climate domains, including Connectedness, Leadership, Safety, Capability and Work Design, also remained generally consistent across waves. Psychosocial hazards such as Management Support, Recognition and Workload displayed small fluctuations but did not show strong changes over time. So, the findings suggest that workforce experiences in this industry remained broadly steady during the study period, with only minor quarter to quarter variation across the key mental health and organisational indicators.
